Retrospective -3 L's  (Liked, Lacked, Learned)

 

The 3Ls is a great data gathering activity which emphasizes learning, one of the 3 Ls. The other Ls stand for: liked,  and lacked.

Running the activity:

  1. Split the canvas in 3 areas:
  •     Liked – things you really liked
  •     Learned – things you have learned
  •     Lacked – things you have missed
  1. Ask the participants to individually write notes on post-it for each of the L areas.
  2. Group conversation about the notes

Below is a sample photo from running this activity. The below inputs from Team ELM for reflection of the sprint efforts for CTP Sprint 23

Liked -

  •  XLR related stories are completed and the sessions with XLR team helped to close on the clarifications
  •  Good Team work and appreciate the help from Navin 
  •  All of the stories planned in the Sprint are completed, except the ones that had dependencies which got moved to next sprint and backlog
  • Team worked on the documentation for the stories completed in the Sprint


Lacked -

  •  Khai moving out will have impact on the automation


Learned -

  •  Dependencies with other teams and requirements are to be cleared before the start of the Sprint
  •  Team need Focus time during the Sprint so that they can complete the assigned stories (No meetings/urgent requests from other dependent teams)
  •  Backlog refinement to be done in consideration with team inputs. 
  • There should not be last minute assignment of stories to the team in the on-going Sprint
  • PO should be available during the Sprint Review/Demo so that the team can walkthrough the stories completed and PO can accept the Stories
